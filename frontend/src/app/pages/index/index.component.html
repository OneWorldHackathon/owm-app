What is the One World Marathon?

One World Strong Foundation embodies the core principles of connection and co-operation, supporting the belief that people around the world should work together toward common goals.

To reinforce that message, we’ve proposed a marathon, where teams of participants from around the world will combine their efforts to achieve a total of 26.2 miles (or 42.195km) over a four-day event. Participants apply and sign-up for a distance they will contribute on the event dates.

Who can participate?

All are welcome, but we especially encourage young people to participate through their schools, colleges and universities, to reinforce the value of making connections, showing curiosity toward other cultures and collaborating toward common goals.

The event is open to all abilities, assisted or unassisted, mobility impaired, teams or individual participants. We aim to have someone from every country in the world participate.

What’s your ultimate aim from the event?

One World Marathon's main objective is to connect people across borders and diverse backgrounds.  We encourage teams that represent multiple continents, countries, and cultures. We hope to inspire people to see the benefits of working together as a global community and encourage others to develop similar projects.

In the long term, we aim to implement a strong, unified, virtual platform, whose message of connectivity and openness contradicts the basic premises of racism, bigotry, violent extremist and terrorist rhetoric. Furthermore, we endeavor to promote a healthy and active lifestyle, global interconnectivity and resiliency for individuals and communities impacted by trauma.

When is it happening?

Between April 12th - April 15th

The One World Marathon will start on April 12th with the RunJordan Dead Sea Ultra Marathon at 6am and it will end when the Boston Marathon’s final participant, and our Global Race Director Dave McGillivray crosses the finish line – approximately midnight EST on April 15th.

How can I participate

It’s up to you!

Simply fill in the form below to enter. How you achieve your distance is up to you!

All participants will be part of a team. The minimum number needed to create a team is 2 participants, there is no maximum number. If your team goes over the 26.2 miles that is OK!

Your distance can be achieved by any type of movement that you are capable of: walk, run, roll over the course of the 4-day event. Participants may be assisted or unassisted. If an individual is assisted, both participants may count their distances and register as separate teammates. A participant may also join multiple teams and contribute separate distances to each team.

How can I follow the progress of the event?

The OWM website and social media platforms will be tracking progress and participation globally.

We encourage participants to share their stories of preparing for the event, and how they overcome challenges along the way. In the lead-up to OWM weekend, our social media channels will feature stories of teamwork, bonding, resilience and the one-on-one connections the teams form pursuing mutual goals.

What will happen after the event?

In the years to come, we aim to build a curriculum with the focus of cultural immersion, global citizenship and resiliency culminating in an annual One World Marathon each Spring, and to expand involvement by increasing the number of education networks and participants on a diverse global scale.
